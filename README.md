# FreedomSound - Another iOS music player

FreedomSound is a music player for iOS. No less, no more. Efficient & straight to the point

## Why?

Mostly because I wasn't fully satisfied how music apps work. As an avid music fan, I believe some songs cannot be classified under one genre only. It is generally not the case for genres that passed the test of time (rock, jazz, variety) but I find it particularly true for EDM songs where the frontier is blurry (and sometimes there isn't a barrier at all). As a result, it is sometimes hard to create playlists.

Another big factor for me (that I'm beginning to see nowadays in other apps) is the **mood** a song belongs to. They're starting to implement it, but as music is very subjective, I find that sometimes the mood described does not fit exactly how I feel the song.

What if a song is mostly drum&bass but has flagship dubstep elements in it? What if I want a playlist that only has house & color bass in it, for a *motivated* mood?

I also found multiple instances where the song I was looking for wasn't in classic music apps like Spotify or SoundCloud. Sometimes it was there but was then deleted, or it simply didn't exist outside of YouTube.

Lastly, I was a Spotify customer for years and years but as my taste in music evolved, Spotify didn't. It was fine for popular songs, but less for more *niche* genres/subgenres. Soundcloud was what I needed in terms of diversity, but it also didn't have some of the songs I had in Spotify. So I grew tired of this and decided that instead of paying 2 subscriptions for the same service but not the same catalog, I'd do my own app. And it would also give me freedom on how **I** handle songs, their genres/subgenres and moods.

## Features

### Existing

Lots of features need to be added, but for now I'm happy with what I have:
- List of all local songs on my phone
- Reads playlist I manually add in the same folder
- Classic features of any music app: Repeat all/one, shuffle, previous/next, play/pause
- Displays metadata (title, artist(s), artwork)
- Working in the background/when phone locked, with Lock Screen/Command Center player
- Satisfying UI for now (simple but polished/appealing)

### Needs improvements

- The playlist system/list. I have lots of plans for it, but the focus for now is the app in itself.
- Reducing bugs and fixing behaviours.
- The UI. Even though it's enough for now, it still feels empty.

## Was IA used in this?

Of course. I'm a TypeScript/JavaScript Full stack dev, and I don't know Swift. Even though I started writing the foundation myself, and I find similarities with React, enabling IA on this project made me progress way faster. And since I didn't intend to put it in public at first, I didn't really care if I couldn't read the code. As said before, maybe I'll change my mind and put proper attention of what IA produces if this picks up momentum/interest.
